About Intermediate CAs

What is an Intermediate CA?

An Intermediate CA sits between the Root CA and end-entity certificates. It is signed by a Root CA (or another Intermediate) and is used to sign server certificates and other Intermediate CAs.

Why use Intermediate CAs?

  • Security - Keeps Root CA offline and protected
  • Flexibility - Different departments can have their own CAs
  • Revocation - Easier to revoke without affecting Root
  • Compliance - Industry best practice for PKI

Best Practices:

  • Use Intermediate CAs for day-to-day signing
  • Limit validity to 3-5 years
  • Use strong key algorithms (RSA 4096 or ECDSA P-384)
  • Consider separate Intermediates for different purposes
